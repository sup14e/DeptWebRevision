Graduate Employment Trends

To effectively track the career paths and future plans of our graduates, we have designed an Exit Survey for graduating students. Additionally, the university's Career Development Center, in collaboration with the  Ministry of Education, conducts follow-up surveys one, three, and five years after graduation. The Office of Institutional Research assists in analyzing the results, supplemented by data from 104 Job Bank, to provide insights into the post-graduation development of both undergraduate and graduate students.

Exit Survey Statistics

Undergraduate Program

According to Figure 5-1, most undergraduate students choose to pursue further education after graduation. Figure 5-2presents a bar chart illustrating graduates' choices between employment and further study. The majority of those pursuing higher education enroll in graduate programs at National Central University, indicating a high retention rate of students continuing their studies at their alma mater.

Graduate (Master’s & Ph.D.) Programs

Based on Figure 5-3, most Master's and Ph.D. graduates choose to enter the workforce, followed by those serving in the military. Only a small portion pursue further studies.  Figure 5-4 shows that most graduates are still job hunting at the time of graduation, while those employed tend to work as software engineers. Company details and positions are shown in Appendix 5-1. Figure 5-5 highlights that students graduating before 2017 often opted for R&D substitute military service, which not only enhanced work experience but also made post-service employment easier.

One-Year Post-Graduation Employment Survey
by the Office of Institutional Research

This survey targets graduates from academic years 2012–2016 (101–105 in ROC calendar) across bachelor's, master's, and Ph.D. programs. Detailed results are available in Appendix 5-2.  Table 5-1 shows the number of graduates surveyed each year and the number of valid responses collected. Data analysis is supported by the Career Development Center.

Employment Status by Degree Program

  • Undergraduate: 79.5% were not employed immediately after graduation, mostly due to continuing their studies.
  • Master’s graduates: 85.76% had full-time jobs within a year; 14.24% did not, mainly due to military service.
  • Professional Master’s Program: 98.36% had full-time employment as students were already working professionals.
  • Ph.D. Program: 81.82% were employed full-time, with most studying part-time while working.

Occupational Fields of Graduates

According to Figure 5-6, most employed graduates work in information technology (IT) fields. A portion also work in STEM (science, technology, engineering, mathematics) related roles.  Table 5-3 details employment types by year and degree level, showing that roughly 70% of graduates across all programs consistently work in IT-related jobs.

Job Search Duration

From Table 5-4, the majority of undergraduate and Master’s graduates secure employment within one month  of graduation. Only about 15% take over six months, often due to military service. Professional Master’s and Ph.D. students typically already have jobs while studying; others also find employment within a month. This indicates strong competitiveness of graduates in the job market.

Job Location Distribution

Table 5-5 reveals that most graduates work in the IT industry, with job locations concentrated in Northern Taiwan. The highest percentages are:

  • Taipei City: 33.87%
  • New Taipei City: 20.59%
  • Taoyuan City: 17.16%
  • Hsinchu City: 12.36%

104 Job Bank – Further Study and Employment Maps

Undergraduate Statistics

Further Study Map: As of August 2019, about 61% of undergraduates pursued further education, with nearly  50% remaining at NCU for graduate studies. Around 34% did not pursue further studies immediately.

Employment Statistics (Table 5-6):

  • Recent graduates (0–2 years) and those 2–5 years post-graduation: ~65% work in IT, mainly in software services (15%).
  • Graduates 5+ years out: Over 70% remain in IT fields, notably in software services and IC design (30%).
  • Around 10% have advanced to supervisory roles.

Graduate (Master’s) Statistics

Employment Statistics (Table 5-7):

  • Recent Master’s graduates: ~85% employed in IT, with 15% in software services.
  • Graduates 5+ years out: Over 70% remain in IT fields, notably in software services and IC design (30%).2+ years post-graduation: ~80% still in IT, mainly in software services and IC design (nearly 30%).
  • 5+ years post-graduation: ~30% have reached supervisory positions. More detailed data can be found in Appendix 5-3.
