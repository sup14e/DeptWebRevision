Revised Graduation Regulations

  • Revised Explanation on 107.10.09
    1. For students who have published papers, obtained patents, or presented works through competitions or exhibitions, and who have passed the subjects recognized by the qualification examination for exemption, the timeline for achieving these results is relaxed. It is no longer restricted to after entering the department. However, it is explicitly required that the work must be published under the full name of the department.
    2. It is explicitly stipulated that students can use published papers to waive the qualification exam, and the author order for the review of works is also specified. This regulation is stricter than before the revision and will be applied to students admitted starting from the 107th academic year.

  • 106.03.15 Revision Explanation

    The revision raises the course credit exemption threshold to 90 points and changes it to "one subject must meet the requirement" for eligibility. This revision applies retroactively to courses taken from the first semester of the 105th academic year.

  • 105.01.13 Revision Explanation

  • 104.06.24 Revision Explanation
    1. The qualification exam subjects have been revised to allow students to select and pass any two subjects. This applies to doctoral students admitted in the 104th academic year (inclusive) and thereafter.
    2. A new pathway for graduation through industry-academia collaboration has been introduced. Please refer to the downloadable files for detailed application forms.
    3. For students graduating through paper publication, the thesis review now includes papers published in top international conferences.

  • 101.06.20 Revision Explanation

    The threshold for passing the thesis review has been raised to two papers. This applies to doctoral students admitted in the 101st academic year (inclusive) and thereafter.


  • 100.01.12 Revision Explanation

    The policy of automatic dismissal for not passing the research proposal defense has been abolished to comply with academic regulations.


  • The department's Academic Committee generally accepts applications for thesis reviews and qualification exam exemptions in the 2nd, 8th, and 14th weeks of each semester. The exact application periods for each semester are subject to the latest announcements on the department’s website.

  • The regulations for exemption of qualification exam subjects in the Doctoral Program have been reviewed and approved by the department's Academic Committee and faculty meetings. The operational details are as follows:
    1. Students who fail to pass the qualification exam within the deadline must submit an application for exemption of the unpassed subjects by the end of the fifth semester.
    2. The application for exemption will generally follow the same timeline as the thesis review application process, with exemption applications accepted during the 2nd, 8th, and 14th weeks of each semester.
    3. The recognized exemption courses are Advanced Algorithms or Algorithms (exempt from the "Algorithms" exam), Operating Systems (exempt from the "Operating Systems" exam), and Computer Architecture (exempt from the "Computer Architecture" exam). To apply for exemption based on course grades, the course must be completed with a grade before the application is submitted.
