ABOUT US

The Department of Computer Science and Information Engineering (CSIE) at National Central University (NCU) was established in 1981 with the initiation of its graduate program. In 1988, the Ph.D. program began enrolling students. The undergraduate program was launched in 1992, completing a comprehensive academic system in computer science education and research. Currently, the department hosts 445 undergraduate students, 247 master’s students, 93 in-service master’s students, and 97 Ph.D. candidates. By 2014, the department had produced 1,399 undergraduate alumni, 1,291 master's graduates, 240 in-service master's graduates, and 157 Ph.D. alumni. Thanks to the efforts of faculty, staff, students, and alumni, the department has earned a distinguished reputation within Taiwan’s research universities.

DEPARTMENT
HISTORY

NCU’s graduate institute of CSIE was founded in 1981. The Ph.D. program was added in 1988 to train high-level researchers and educators. The undergraduate program began in 1992 to strengthen basic IT education. In 1996, the undergraduate program expanded to two classes per year to meet increasing industry demands. In 2001, the in-service master’s program was introduced to provide further education for professionals. In 2005, in response to policies from the Ministry of Economic Affairs, an embedded systems industry-focused master's program was established to bridge academia and industry. In 2006, recognizing the need for systematic software engineering education in Taiwan, the country’s first Graduate Institute of Software Engineering was launched. It shares resources with the CSIE department but also develops its own distinct goals and curriculum.

ACADEMIC
FEATURES

  • Each required and elective course is assigned a teaching assistant; required courses also include Ph.D. student TAs.
  • Introduction to CS course covers the department’s four main research areas, related industries, and foundational knowledge.
  • Robotics and programming courses are offered to undergraduates, integrating hands-on platforms from freshman year.
  • Modular hardware-software platforms are self-developed and used in digital systems, embedded software, and system design courses.
  • An early warning system is in place for students struggling academically.
  • End-of-term course evaluations help improve teaching quality.
  • Each class is assigned two academic advisors who provide tutoring and group meetings.
  • Advisor meeting logs help track guidance effectiveness.
  • Advisors meet with each student at least five times per semester.
  • Students are encouraged to engage in community service, tutoring students in rural or underserved areas.
  • Annual student music recitals are held to showcase talents.
  • TA feedback sessions recognize outstanding performance and share experiences.
  • Learning surveys are conducted in week 6 of each semester to guide course adjustments.

TRANSPORTATION

By Car

NCU is located near the Zhongli Interchange on National Freeway No.1 (Exit 62, toward Xinwu). Parking on campus costs NT$30 per hour. Parking is available near Engineering Building V, home of the CSIE department.

By Train

From Zhongli Train Station, take a bus or taxi to NCU.
Train schedule: https://tip.railway.gov.tw/tra-tip-web/tip?lang=EN_US.

By High-Speed Rail

Get off at HSR Taoyuan Station:

  • Take Bus 132 or 172 directly to NCU.
  • Or transfer in Zhongli City by bus.
  • Taxis are available at the HSR station (Taiwan Taxi: 4058-8888 or mobile 55688).
  • Driving from HSR station to NCU takes about 15–20 minutes.
HSR schedule: https://www.thsrc.com.tw/en/home.

CAMPUS
LANDMARKS

NCU Archway

The iconic archway at the main entrance is known as the "NCU Triumphal Arch." The 600-meter central road leads directly into campus.

Turtle Pond

Located in front of Dormitory 9, the fountain pond is home to many turtles basking in the sun. Its shimmering fountain is a quiet and scenic spot.

Lover’s Lane

A scenic 2 kilo meter pine-lined trail around campus, wide enough for two people to walk side by side—hence the nickname.

Tai Chi Sculpture

Located on the lawn near the College of Liberal Arts, this sculpture by Ju Ming, a master of contemporary art, represents traditional Chinese culture and serves as a peaceful rest area for students and visitors.
